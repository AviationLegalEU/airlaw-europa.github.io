1. Relation with other legal instruments

1.1. Montreal Convention 1999

The right based on article 7 of Regulation 261/2004 is a passengers rights to a standardised and lump-sum payement following the cancellation of a flight, a right which is independennt of compensation for damage in the context of article 19 of the Montreal Convention ⏵ CJEU 10 January 2006, C-344/04, IATA v. Department of Transport - 2006 ECR I-403

The passenger who accepts a set-off compensation based on article 7 of the Regulation is barred from claiming damages under the Montreal Convention ⏵ AG Frankfurt am Main, vom 11.01.2017 31 C 1209/16 (83) - openJur 2019, 32475

1.2 Regulation 1215/2012 "Brussels 1 Bis"

(i) Competence of jurisdictions in air transport contract

In the case of air transport of passengers from one Member State to another Member State, carried on the basis of a contract with only one airlinne, which is the operating carrier, the court having jurisdiction to deal with a claim for compensation founded on that transport contract and on regulation 261/2004 is that which has territorial jurisdiction over the place of departure or place of arrival of the aircraft as stated in the contract ⏵ CJEU 9 July 2009, C-204/08, Peter Rehder v. Air Baltic Corporation - 2009 ECR I-06073

...and does not have necessary close link to the contract the place of the registered office of the air carrier... nor its principal place of establishment... and the same is true for the place of conclusion of the contract for air transport or the place where the ticket is issued... or a stopover of the aircraft
